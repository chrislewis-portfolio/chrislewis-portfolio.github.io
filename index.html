1. 2015 Robot Scoring Mechanism - Sketch, CAD Animation, Final

Sketch

During my FRC team’s 2015 season, I designed the main scoring mechanism of our robot. I CAD-ed, machined and built the mechanism that both captured and lifted totes. First, I used a Lego mock up to explain the idea to my team. Then I drew sketches (above) to lay out the components. I designed the mechanism to only require power to move it vertically, so it wouldn’t need any additional motors or servos to operate the capturing mechanism. The “claws” spun freely and were attached to a spring pulling the part down to a hard-stop at the bottom so the totes wouldn’t fall through.  When the robot approached a tote, the mechanism would lower the stack it was previously carrying on top of the tote, catch the lip of the bottom tote, then lift the entire stack of totes, as shown in the video.

CAD Animation (Video)

After making a mock-up and sketch, I used CAD to fully design my mechanism. I had to make changes from my initial sketch. To reduce the robots weight, I changed my design from using 4 support beams to only 2. Because the gearbox for the ball screw was taller than we expected, I had to move the claws down relative to the ball nut. This is an animation of the CAD assembly I made, showing the kinematics of the parts.

Practice Run (Video)

This video is a short clip of a practice run. The claws go down over the lip of the totes. When the mechanism is raised, the claws are stopped from letting the totes fall through. The robot then picks up more totes and delivers them to the scoring platform.

At Tournament (Image)

This image shows our robot at competition, scoring a stack of totes and a recycling bin onto the white platform.

2. 2016 Belt Drive Train - CAD Rendering, Prototype and Assembled Product

CAD Rendering

This is my CAD rendering from Autodesk Inventor of the drive train I designed for my FTC team’s 2016 robot. Using individual CAD parts that we downloaded from vendor sites of our purchased pieces, I made an assembly model. A motor drives a sprocket, which turns another sprocket attached to the drive pulley using chains. A pair of large pulleys then drives the belt. We added an encoder on a idler pulley on the bottom of the belt. In earlier versions of our robot, we experienced slippage on the belt while driving, so I designed a tensioner that ensured constant pressure so our drive pulleys never slipped.

3D Printed Prototype (Image)

To test the tensioner that I designed, I 3D-printed a prototype we could use until we machined it. The tensioner worked by having a spring pull the component to the side. It pivoted around the bottom left hole, resulting in the pulley constantly pushing against the belt so it wouldn’t slip on the drive pulleys.

Being Assembled (Image)

This image shows the drive train mostly assembled, with my machined tensioner, before it was attached to the robot. In this image, the belt isn’t taut because the spring had not yet been secured.

Driving (Video)

This clip shows the tensioner in use on the driving robot. We milled many open gaps in the side plates because we needed to reduce the weight of the robot.

3. Art And Design Pieces

Piranha Plant from Super Mario Bros.

In this project, I designed the Piranha plant from Super Mario Bros. using only primitive solids (cubes, spheres, cylinders, cones, and pyramids). I made a charcoal drawing (left), and to verify that my shadows were accurate, I made a computer rendering using Autodesk Maya (right). I was happy to find they were close.

High Contrast Self Portrait

I hand made this self-portrait collage by drawing dark areas of a high-contrast image of myself on a sheet of Mylar. Underneath the Mylar, I placed cutouts of topographical maps, railroad maps, and binary text to fill the white areas in my face, neck, shirt, and background.

Initial Animation (Video)

Using Cinema 4D, I created a growing spline (curved line in 3D space) that appeared as my initials (“C”, “L”) from two planes. I used the shadow from two lights placed on opposing planes to show the two initials, the letter “C” in the green shadow and the letter “L” in the blue shadow.

Minion Perspective Drawing

I hand drew this three point perspective piece. I colored the rocket car and minion using colored pencils and the blocks using drafting markers and shaded with colored pencils.

Blind Contour

In this blind contour, I covered the paper from my sight while drawing my friend’s face, using a single, continuous line. I went over the initial pencil contour with ink, and then I added a tessellation pattern in the hair and added dots in a hexagonal pattern to balance the drawing.
